What is a network?

Group activity: What is a network?

Please...

  • Divide into four groups, one in each corner of the room (make sure you're in a group with at least some people you don't know).
  • You two minutes to write down up to five definitions "network", and put them on post-it notes on the wall.
  • Once complete, move to the next group clockwise, and discuss their definitions. Did the other group write anything you didn't think of?
  • At the end, one person from each group will give a 1 minute summary of their own group's definitions, and the differences between their definitions and what the other group produced.

There are tonnes of different definitions!

This week's readings...

Main readings:

Castells, Manuel. "Communication, power and counter-power in the network society." International journal of communication 1.1 (2007): 29.
Massey, D. (1991). A Global Sense of Place. Marxism Today, 35, 315-323. http://doi.org/10.1016/j.pecs.2007.10.001

Taking into consideration what we read this week, in groups, consider what networks you are a part of. How do you

a) use,

b) create and

c) disrupt them?

You have five minutes. After that, you'll have the chance to discuss your answers whilst we go through this week's readings together.

Castells (2007, p252):

The distinction between mass media and mass self-communication has analytical value, but only on the condition to add that the two modes of communication are interacting in the practice of communication, as communication tecnologies converge.

What does Castells mean by this?

Can we think of any examples today?

Massey (1990):

Different social groups have distinct relationships to this anyway differentiated mobility: some people are more in charge of it than others; some initiate flows and movement, others don't; some are more on the receiving end of it than others; some are effectively imprisoned by it.

How are we positioned in these flows and differentiated mobility?

Are we initiators? Receivers? Prisoners? One or two? All three?

A little food for thought...

Something to think about over the course of this week.

Make sure you've read the paper by Doreen Massey "A Global Sense of Place".

You've just started at King's College London, and maybe just moved into a new environment. You've probably met lots of new people, and set up lots of new networks (new friends, social media, new amenities, etc.).

Take a few minutes at some point during the week to reflect, and maybe discuss, how have your networked technologies affected your new sense of place?
