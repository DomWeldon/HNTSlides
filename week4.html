TCP/IP

Packet Switching vs Circuit Switching

Circuit Switching

A continuous, dedicated circuit exists between any two devices that connect to eachother, regardless of whether the devices are communicating at that time.

Most conventional telephone networks were circuit switched (less so today).

If the internet were circuit switched, every computer would have to have a channel or a wire to every other computer it wanted to talk to.

This wouldn't really work....

Packet Switching

Packets are routed through the network

Packets have a from address and a to address, and are marked with their sending time

The packets contain a portion of a message, just like the contents of a parcel (about 1,400 bytes)

Packets can travel along any route through a network

The portions of a message in a parcel are reconstructed at the other end to form a full message

How to Play

I need at least three people (or pairs) to be computers and sit at their desks. You are numbered 0 to 100.

Everyone else should take a card and become a router. Sit in the middle of the desks. Your card will give you a number between 0 and 100, this is your address. The second number, between 1 and 3 is your degree, this is the number of connections to routers you are allowed to make. Choose the other routers you are making your connections to now.

Computers, choose one router to connect to now.

Computers (hosts) write messages, three words at a time, and hand them to their router. Routers will route the message through the network to other routers, until the message reaches its intended computer. When a router receives a message, if it is to an even-numbere computer, pass it to an even-numbered router router, and vice versa for odd numbers.

Winner is the first computer to ask a question and receive a response from all other computers.
