Net Neutrality

All data on the internet should be treated equally.

Service providers and government regulators should not prioritize data from one location over data from another.

You should be able to access google.com just as easily as bing.com

OR amazon.com just as easily as any other web shop.

Amazon.com can't pay your ISP to block or slow down another site.

How the internet works, shouldn't be affected by the data or content it is carrying.

Google-ization.

The internet is very pervasive in our everyday lives. We use it to understand everything from where we are, to recall basic factual, and often contensted, information.

How much are we in control of the itnernet, and how much does the internet control us?

Networking Power power of the actors and organizations included in the networks that constitute the core of the global network society over human collectives and individuals who are not included in these global networks
Network Power power resulting from the standards required to coordinate social interaction in the networks. In this case, power is exercised not by exclusion from the networks but by the imposition of the rules of inclusion.
Networked Power power of social actors over other social actors in the network (specific to each network).
Network-making Power power to program specific networks according to the interests and values of the programmers, and the power to switch different networks following the strategic alliances between the dominant actors of various networks.
