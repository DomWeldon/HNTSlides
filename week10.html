All data on the internet should be treated equally.

Service providers and government regulators should not prioritize data from one location over data from another.

You should be able to access google.com just as easily as bing.com

OR amazon.com just as easily as any other web shop.

Amazon.com can't pay your ISP to block or slow down another site.

How the internet works, shouldn't be affected by the data or content it is carrying.

There are different conceptions of net neutrality.

Most commonly revolve around the idea of 'fast lanes' on the internet, or allowing content companies to subsidise service providers.

NY Times Video

But why shouldn't ISPs be allowed to sell fast access to their internet?

What about the developing world?
